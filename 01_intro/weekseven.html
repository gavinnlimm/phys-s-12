Electronic Output Devices

In week 7 of our class, we delved into an exciting array of topics related to electronics and microcontrollers. The week began with a thorough review of our previous assignments, offering valuable feedback and addressing any questions raised by the students. We then dived into a Servo Motor Workshop, where we learned how to control these precise motors using microcontrollers like the ESP32. Another significant aspect of our learning was understanding the use of an oscilloscope, a crucial tool in electronics, to visualize electrical signals effectively. During a well-deserved break, we utilized our time to install essential drivers for ESP32 and/or ESP32-CAM microcontrollers. However, the highlight of the week was the Output Device Extravaganza, where we got hands-on experience choosing from various output devices, including RGB LEDs, LED Strips, OLED/TFT displays, Piezo Buzzers, and even EMF devices like solenoids. Through these practical exercises, we honed our skills in interfacing, circuit control, and programming, enabling us to create intriguing projects with the microcontrollers and other electronic components.
